Racist attacks in Italy since the elections:

A timeline

2018 has been a particularly virulent year in Italian politics, as racially motivated attacks towards migrants have multiplied throughout the peninsula. This timeline helps keeping track of what's happening.

by Viola Serena Stefanello

On Februrary 3rd, 2018, the Italian town of Macerata turned into the scene of a terrorist attack: 28-year-old Luca Traini fired his gun, voluntarily wounding six African migrants. The attacker eveloped himself in an Italian flag and shouted "Long live Italy!" before surrendering to the police. He was soon recognised as a far-right sympathizer.

Since then, and especially after the March 2018 elections that saw nationalist party Lega raise to the government in a colation with populist Movimento Cinque Stelle, racially motivated attacks have multiplied beyond proportion. Just in the first three months of the year, 169 cases of racis were counted in Italy.

Over fifty violent attacks towards migrants and refugees have been registered since arch 4th, when the elections took place. This timeline tracks these events to make a sense of the situation in the country.

04/03

Italian elections: Movimento 5 Stelle is the most voted party, but nationalist party Lega, lead by Matteo Salvini, reaches the all-time high result of 17%.

05/03, Florence

54-years-old Senegalese Idy Diene is shot and killed by a pensioner. The killer said he just wanted to kill someone randomly.

09/04, Trento

Someone sets on fire the door of a bed and breakfast that was supposed to host some refugees.

19/05, Bolzano

A bomb explodes outside a refugee centre. The attackers leave a board with swastikas and celtic crosses on it.

02/06, Vibo Valentia

Souamaila Sacko, an activist, is killed. Other two migrants are also wounded in the same attack.

11/06, Caserta

Two refugees seekers from Mali are shot with an airgun. The attackers, three Italians, dedicate the act to Salvini.

20/06, Napoli

A Malian refugee seeker is wounded with an airgun. "They laughed while they shot me", he told the police.
